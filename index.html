Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our role as your connectivity procurement specialist for DIA, wavelength, and dark fiber services.

Dedicated Internet Access (DIA) gives you a managed, symmetrical internet circuit with a defined bandwidth (for example, 1 Gbps) and an SLA. It is ideal for offices and sites where you need predictable connectivity to the public internet and cloud services without managing the underlay yourself.

Wavelength services provide point-to-point optical capacity between two locations (such as data centers) at fixed rates like 10 Gbps, 100 Gbps, or 400 Gbps. The carrier manages the optical equipment, and you simply consume the capacity as if it were a very high-speed private line.

Dark fiber gives you access to unused fiber strands that you light with your own optical equipment, giving you maximum control and scalability but requiring more internal expertise and capex. Awantrix helps you evaluate your locations, traffic patterns, and growth expectations to recommend the right mix of DIA, wavelength, and dark fiber for your environment.

We maintain direct relationships and API/portal integrations with hundreds of global, regional, and local network operators. When you share your site locations and requirements, our team and the Claude (Anthropic) AI engine generate standardized requests that we route across appropriate providers based on geography, service type, and capacity.

For common metropolitan and data center locations, we can typically return preliminary quote options within 24–72 hours. More complex projects involving new builds, long-haul routes, or dark fiber can take longer, but our process usually compresses procurement cycles from traditional 8–12 weeks down to around 5–10 business days for a decision-ready comparison.

Awantrix operates as a carrier-neutral connectivity procurement specialist. We work with a broad ecosystem of providers and are not beholden to any single carrier's product quota or sales target. Our role is to align providers and routes to your architectural and commercial requirements.

For each project, we clearly document how many providers were evaluated, which were shortlisted, and why. We highlight trade-offs between price, latency, diversity, support history, and contract terms so you can make an informed decision. Our incentives are aligned with building a long-term partnership with you, not maximizing short-term revenue from any one carrier.

Ongoing support means that Awantrix remains your first point of contact for all connectivity we help you procure. Our team coordinates with carriers for incident triage, fault escalation, RFOs, and SLA credit claims, reducing the burden on your operations team.

We also help manage MACD (move, add, change, delete) requests, contract renewals, capacity upgrades, and diversification projects over time. Instead of juggling multiple portals and account managers, you get a single, consistent experience and a holistic view of your bandwidth services across DIA, wavelength, and dark fiber.

In most cases, Awantrix is compensated by carriers as an authorized partner, similar to how they compensate their direct sales channels. Because we aggregate demand and maintain strong commercial relationships across many providers, we are often able to secure pricing and terms that are equal to or better than what you would obtain by going direct—without adding margin on top of carrier quotes.

We are completely transparent about commercial structures and will work with you and your procurement team to ensure our model fits your policies. The majority of our customers see a net reduction in total connectivity spend once we optimize routes, contracts, and bandwidth tiers, even after our involvement is factored in.
